## GRADE METHODOLOGY FOR HERBAL EVIDENCE

### Quality Levels
- **Grade A (High):** Further research unlikely to change effect estimate. Usually requires multiple RCTs with consistent effects, large sample sizes, direct outcomes.
  - **For herbal:** Rare; requires 5+ quality RCTs with n>100 each, consistent findings

- **Grade B (Moderate):** Further research may change effect estimate. Usually RCTs with some limitations, OR multiple smaller trials.
  - **For herbal:** 3-4 well-designed RCTs (n>50-100), consistent effects, low heterogeneity

- **Grade C (Low):** Further research very likely to change estimate. Small RCTs, observational studies, inconsistent results.
  - **For herbal:** Fewer RCTs (1-2), small sample sizes, variable outcomes, high heterogeneity

- **Grade D (Very Low):** Any estimate uncertain. Mechanism studies, animal studies, case series.
  - **For herbal:** No human RCTs, only mechanistic/animal data

### Assessment Domains
1. **Risk of Bias** — Study design, randomization quality, blinding adequacy, attrition
2. **Inconsistency** — Heterogeneity across studies (I²), effect direction consistency
3. **Indirectness** — Population relevance, outcome relevance, setting relevance
4. **Imprecision** — Confidence intervals wide? Sample size adequate?
5. **Publication Bias** — Selective outcome reporting, missing negative studies?

## KEY INSIGHTS FOR VITALORA IMPLEMENTATION

### Strengths of Herbal Evidence Base
✅ 50+ RCTs identified with DOI links
✅ Consistent effects for curcumin, green tea, garlic, ginger, olive leaf (Grade B)
✅ Excellent safety profile across all remedies
✅ Indonesian population context studies available
✅ Mechanisms partially validated in humans

### Limitations Requiring Transparency
⚠️ Mostly Grade B (not Grade A) — smaller sample sizes than pharmaceutical trials
⚠️ Publication bias likely — negative small studies underreported
⚠️ Standardization varies — active compound content differs by preparation
⚠️ Individual response variability — responder/non-responder phenotypes exist
⚠️ Long-term safety — most RCTs <6 months duration

### Vitalora Transparency Approach
1. **Grade badges:** Display GRADE rating (B vs. C) on every herbal recommendation
2. **Effect size transparency:** Show actual % biomarker change from RCTs
3. **DOI links:** Every herbal claim linked to source paper with full citation
4. **Mechanism labels:** Distinguish "validated in humans" vs. "proposed mechanism"
5. **Safety warnings:** Drug interaction alerts + contraindication checker
