## GRADE Methodology Overview

### GRADE Quality Levels
- **Grade A (High Quality):** Further research unlikely to change effect estimate
  - **Criteria:** RCTs without serious limitations, multiple consistent studies, large effects, low heterogeneity

- **Grade B (Moderate Quality):** Further research may change effect estimate
  - **Criteria:** Downgrade from high due to: RCT with limitations, cohort studies, inconsistent results, imprecision

- **Grade C (Low Quality):** Further research very likely to change estimate
  - **Criteria:** Case-control studies, observational studies only, serious limitations, small sample sizes

- **Grade D (Very Low Quality):** Any estimate very uncertain
  - **Criteria:** Case reports, expert opinion, mechanistic studies, severe methodological flaws

### Assessment Domains
1. **Risk of Bias** (Study design quality, confounding control, attrition)
2. **Inconsistency** (Homogeneity of results across studies, heterogeneity I²)
3. **Indirectness** (Relevance to target population/outcomes/setting)
4. **Imprecision** (Wide confidence intervals, small sample sizes, adequate power)
5. **Publication Bias** (Funnel plot asymmetry, selective outcome reporting)
