❤️‍🩹 Hypertension (3)
HTN-001: 2017 ACC/AHA Guidelines
Whelton PK, Carey RM (2017) | Hypertension
GRADE A
Clinical guideline based on 600+ studies. Transparent GRADE methodology. SBP ≥130 threshold grounded in outcome trials including SPRINT. Evidence-based definition updates.
HTN-002: Framingham Heart Study
Kannel WB, et al. (2002-2020) | Hypertension
GRADE A
Landmark prospective cohort (8,000+ subjects, 44-year follow-up). Dose-dependent BP-CVD relationship. Framingham Risk Score: 10-year CVD calculator. 8,500+ citations. Reproducible across populations.
HTN-003: SPRINT Trial RCT
SPRINT Research Group (2015) | NEJM
GRADE A
N=9,361 multicenter RCT. Intensive SBP <120 vs <140 reduces CVD 27%. Low risk of bias, adequate power, clearly defined outcomes. Direct influence on 2017 guideline revisions.
🏥 NAFLD (3)
NAFLD-001: EASL Guideline 2016
EASL Committee (2016) | J Hepatology
GRADE A
Evidence-based guideline. Systematic evaluation of non-invasive fibrosis methods. FIB-4 index recommended first-line (AUROC 0.81). Transparent methodology with evidence-graded recommendations.
NAFLD-002: FIB-4 Validation
Shah AG, et al. (2009) | Hepatology
GRADE A
Cross-sectional validation with histologically confirmed NAFLD (gold standard). FIB-4 AUROC 0.81-0.88. Large sample sizes, stable estimates. 950+ citations indicate broad validation.
NAFLD-003: IR Pathophysiology Review
Stefan N, et al. (2018) | Nat Rev
GRADE B
Narrative review of IR mechanisms in NAFLD-MetS-DM. Limited by review design (not systematic). Mechanisms well-established. HOMA-IR and dietary intervention evidence (low GI, fiber) well-supported.
📈 Metabolic Syndrome (3)
MET-001: IDF Definition 2019
IDF Consensus Group (2019)
GRADE A
International consensus definition. 5-component diagnostic criteria (central obesity + ≥2 of HTN/dyslipidemia/hyperglycemia/low HDL). 3,900+ citations. Links to 2-4x CVD risk, 5-9x diabetes risk. Universal acceptance.
MET-002: MetS & CVD Meta-Analysis
Mottillo S, et al. (2010) | JACC
GRADE A
Meta-analysis of 87 studies, 900,000+ subjects. MetS increases CVD 2.35x (95% CI 2.02-2.74). Mortality increased 1.52x. Risk driven by components, not diagnosis alone. Robust evidence.
MET-003: PREDIMED Diet Trial
Estruch R, et al. (2013) | NEJM
GRADE A
Multicenter RCT, N=7,447 (4.8-year follow-up). Mediterranean diet reduces CVD 30% in MetS/high-risk. Primary prevention evidence. Emphasizes olive oil, nuts, whole grains, legumes.
🩺 Type 2 Diabetes (3)
DM2-001: DCCT/EDIC Control Trial
DCCT/EDIC Group (1993-2020) | NEJM
GRADE A
Landmark RCT (N=1,441). Intensive glycemic control (HbA1c <7%) reduces neuropathy 54%, nephropathy 63%, retinopathy 76%. HbA1c ≥6.5% diagnostic. Prediabetes reversible with lifestyle. 6,500+ citations.
DM2-002: ADA Standards 2024
American Diabetes Assn (2024) | Diabetes Care
GRADE A
Clinical guidelines. T2DM diagnosis: FPG ≥126, OGTT ≥200, HbA1c ≥6.5%. Screening ≥45y or overweight+risk. FINDRISC score for risk identification. Most cited annually. Gold standard.
DM2-003: IR & MetS Definition
Kahn R, et al. (2005) | JCEM
GRADE B
Consensus definition. Insulin resistance central to T2DM pathophysiology. HOMA-IR = (fasting insulin × glucose)/22.5. Linked to obesity, inactivity, dietary quality. Reversible with lifestyle.
