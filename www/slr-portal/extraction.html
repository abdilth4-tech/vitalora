💛 Dyslipidemia (3 Papers)

DYS-001
Familial Hypercholesterolemia is Underdiagnosed and Undertreated
Author: Nordestgaard BG, et al.
Year: 2016
Journal: European Heart Journal
Design: Systematic Review
Population: 12 prospective studies meta-analysis
DOI: 10.1093/eurheartj/ehw145
Key Finding: FH prevalence 1:500 (heterozygous), severely underdiagnosed (only 10% identified). LDL cholesterol >3.0 mmol/L elevated in FH. Dutch Lipid Clinic Network Score diagnostic tool.
GRADE A Risk Stratification Genetic
DYS-002
INTERHEART: A Case-Control Study of MI Risk Factors
Author: Yusuf S, et al.
Year: 2004
Journal: Lancet
Design: Case-Control (27,098 subjects)
Countries: 52 countries
DOI: 10.1016/S0140-6736(04)17018-9
Key Finding: 9 modifiable risk factors account for 90% of MI risk globally. ApoB/A1 ratio primary lipid marker. INTERHEART Risk Score AUROC = 0.87. Dyslipidemia top 3 modifiable risk.
GRADE A Multi-Factor Algorithm CV Risk
DYS-003
Heart Disease & Stroke Statistics 2015
Author: Mozaffarian D, Benjamin EJ, et al.
Year: 2015
Journal: Circulation
Design: Epidemiological Compilation
Studies Meta-Analyzed: 1,000+
DOI: 10.1161/CIR.0000000000000350
Key Finding: Dyslipidemia affects 39% of adults. Dietary factors account for 30-40% of lipid variance. LDL optimal <100, high ≥160 mg/dL. Framingham Risk Score embedded.
GRADE A Epidemiology Dietary Modifiable

❤️‍🩹 Hypertension (3 Papers)

HTN-001
2017 ACC/AHA High Blood Pressure Guidelines
Author: Whelton PK, Carey RM, et al.
Year: 2017
Journal: Hypertension
Design: Clinical Practice Guideline
Studies Reviewed: 600+
DOI: 10.1161/HYP.0000000000000065
Key Finding: HTN definition: SBP ≥130 or DBP ≥80. Intensive control (SBP <120) reduces CVD 27% in high-risk (SPRINT trial). Affects 1.13 billion globally. DASH diet, sodium reduction evidence-based.
GRADE A Diagnostic Threshold Treatment Target
HTN-002
Framingham Heart Study: Hypertension Outcomes
Author: Kannel WB, et al.
Year: 2002-2020 (multiple cohorts)
Journal: Hypertension
Design: Prospective Cohort (44 years)
Population: ~8,000+ subjects
Citations: 8,500+
Key Finding: BP-CVD relationship dose-dependent. Framingham Risk Score: 10-year CVD risk calculator incorporating BP + age + lipids + smoking. Stage 1 HTN (130-139) shows 10-year ASCVD ≥10%.
GRADE A Landmark Cohort Risk Calculator
HTN-003
SPRINT Trial: Intensive BP Control in High-Risk
Author: SPRINT Research Group
Year: 2015
Journal: New England Journal of Medicine
Design: RCT (9,361 subjects)
Outcome: Intensive SBP <120 vs. <140
Result: 27% CVD risk reduction
Key Finding: Intensive BP control (SBP <120) vs. standard (<140): 27% relative CVD risk reduction in high-risk groups. Evidence for aggressive treatment targets. Supports 2017 ACC/AHA redefinition.
GRADE A RCT Evidence Treatment Target

🏥 NAFLD (3 Papers)

NAFLD-001
NAFLD Clinical Guideline: Screening and Management
Author: EASL Guideline Committee
Year: 2016
Journal: Journal of Hepatology
Design: Clinical Guideline
Focus: Non-invasive fibrosis assessment
Tool: FIB-4 index (AUROC 0.81)
Key Finding: FIB-4 index = (Age × AST) / (Platelet × √ALT). AUROC 0.81 for detecting advanced fibrosis. Non-invasive alternative to liver biopsy. Calculated from routine labs.
GRADE A Fibrosis Assessment Non-Invasive
NAFLD-002
FIB-4 Index Validation Study: Advanced Fibrosis Detection
Author: Shah AG, et al.
Year: 2009
Journal: Hepatology
Design: Cross-sectional Validation
Population: NAFLD patients with biopsy
Citations: 950+
Key Finding: FIB-4 Index AUROC 0.81-0.88 for identifying advanced fibrosis. Outperforms APRI (AST-to-platelet). Cost-effective compared to elastography. Broadly applicable.
GRADE A Diagnostic Accuracy Cost-Effective
NAFLD-003
Insulin Resistance as Common Driver of NAFLD-MetS
Author: Stefan N, et al.
Year: 2018
Journal: Nature Reviews
Design: Review Article
Focus: Insulin resistance mechanisms
Grade: B (Review)
Key Finding: Insulin resistance central to NAFLD pathophysiology. Links NAFLD-MetS-Diabetes. Dietary intervention (low GI, increased fiber) targets IR. HOMA-IR marker for risk assessment.
GRADE B Pathophysiology IR Marker

📈 Metabolic Syndrome (3 Papers)

MET-001
IDF Definition of Metabolic Syndrome 2019
Author: IDF Consensus Group
Year: 2019
Organization: International Diabetes Federation
Design: Consensus Definition
Components: 5-factor cluster
Citations: 3,900+
Key Finding: MetS = Central obesity + 2 of: HTN, dyslipidemia, hyperglycemia, low HDL. Increases CVD risk 2-4x, diabetes risk 5-9x. Prevalence ~25% globally.
GRADE A Diagnostic Criteria 5-Component
MET-002
Metabolic Syndrome & Cardiovascular Risk: Meta-Analysis
Author: Mottillo S, et al.
Year: 2010
Journal: Journal of the American College of Cardiology
Design: Meta-Analysis (87 studies)
Population: 900,000+ subjects
Citations: 1,650+
Key Finding: MetS diagnosis increases CVD risk 2.35x (RR 2.35, 95% CI 2.02-2.74). Increases all-cause mortality 1.52x. Risk driven by components, not diagnosis alone.
GRADE A CVD Risk Quantified Meta-Analysis
MET-003
PREDIMED Study: Mediterranean Diet & CVD Prevention
Author: Estruch R, et al.
Year: 2013
Journal: New England Journal of Medicine
Design: Multicenter RCT (7,447 subjects)
Duration: 4.8 years follow-up
Citations: 2,800+
Key Finding: Mediterranean diet reduces CVD events 30% in MetS/at-risk. Primary prevention (not treatment). Emphasizes olive oil, nuts, whole grains, legumes.
GRADE A RCT Dietary 30% Risk Reduction

🩺 Type 2 Diabetes (3 Papers)

DM2-001
DCCT/EDIC: Intensive Glycemic Control & Complications
Author: Diabetes Control & Complications Group
Year: 1993-2020 (ongoing)
Journal: New England Journal of Medicine
Design: Landmark RCT (1,441 subjects)
Outcome: Microvascular complications
Citations: 6,500+
Key Finding: Intensive glycemic control (HbA1c <7%) reduces neuropathy 54%, nephropathy 63%, retinopathy 76%. HbA1c ≥6.5% diagnostic. Prediabetes reversible with lifestyle.
GRADE A RCT Landmark Complication Prevention
DM2-002
ADA Standards of Care 2024: Diagnosis & Screening
Author: American Diabetes Association
Year: 2024
Journal: Diabetes Care
Design: Clinical Guidelines
Topics: Diagnosis, screening, management
Gold Standard: Most cited annually
Key Finding: T2DM diagnosis: FPG ≥126, OGTT ≥200, HbA1c ≥6.5%. Screening recommended ≥45 years or if overweight + risk factors. FINDRISC score for risk identification.
GRADE A Diagnostic Cutoff Screening Guidelines
DM2-003
Insulin Resistance & Metabolic Syndrome Definition
Author: Kahn R, et al.
Year: 2005
Journal: Journal of Clinical Endocrinology & Metabolism
Design: Consensus Definition
Focus: Insulin resistance mechanisms
Grade: B (Review)
Key Finding: Insulin resistance central to T2DM pathophysiology. HOMA-IR = (fasting insulin × glucose)/22.5. Linked to obesity, physical inactivity, dietary quality. Reversible with lifestyle.
GRADE B Pathophysiology HOMA-IR Marker
