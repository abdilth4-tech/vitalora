Structured extraction from 15 landmark peer-reviewed studies across 5 prioritized diseases. Each paper analyzed for biomarkers, diagnostic cutoffs, risk algorithms, and clinical applicability.

💛 Dyslipidemia
DYS-001
Familial Hypercholesterolemia is Underdiagnosed and Undertreated
Author: Nordestgaard BG, et al.
Year: 2016
Journal: Eur Heart J
Design: Systematic Review
Key Finding: FH prevalence 1:500. LDL >3.0 mmol/L elevated. Dutch Lipid Clinic Network Score for diagnosis.
GRADE A Risk Score Genetic
DYS-002
INTERHEART: Case-Control Study of MI Risk Factors
Author: Yusuf S, et al.
Year: 2004
Design: Case-Control, 27,098
Countries: 52 countries
Key Finding: 9 risk factors account for 90% MI risk. ApoB/A1 ratio primary. INTERHEART AUROC = 0.87.
GRADE A Algorithm CV Risk
DYS-003
Heart Disease & Stroke Statistics 2015
Author: Mozaffarian D, Benjamin EJ
Year: 2015
Journal: Circulation
Studies: 1,000+
Key Finding: Dyslipidemia affects 39% of adults. LDL optimal <100, high ≥160 mg/dL. Framingham Risk Score embedded.
GRADE A Epidemiology Modifiable
❤️‍🩹 Hypertension
HTN-001
2017 ACC/AHA High Blood Pressure Guidelines
Author: Whelton PK, Carey RM
Year: 2017
Design: Clinical Guideline
Studies: 600+
Key Finding: HTN: SBP ≥130 or DBP ≥80. Intensive control (SBP <120) reduces CVD 27%. Affects 1.13B globally.
GRADE A Diagnostic Treatment
HTN-002
Framingham Heart Study: Hypertension Outcomes
Author: Kannel WB, et al.
Year: 2002-2020
Design: Prospective Cohort, 44y
Population: ~8,000 subjects
Key Finding: BP-CVD dose-dependent. Framingham Risk Score: 10-year CVD calculator. Stage 1 HTN shows ASCVD ≥10%.
GRADE A Landmark Calculator
HTN-003
SPRINT Trial: Intensive BP Control in High-Risk
Author: SPRINT Research Group
Year: 2015
Journal: NEJM
Design: RCT, 9,361
Key Finding: Intensive SBP <120 vs <140 reduces CVD 27%. Supports 2017 ACC/AHA threshold redefinition.
GRADE A RCT Treatment
🏥 NAFLD
NAFLD-001
NAFLD Clinical Guideline: Screening and Management
Author: EASL Guideline Committee
Year: 2016
Journal: J Hepatology
Focus: Non-invasive fibrosis
Key Finding: FIB-4 = (Age × AST) / (Platelet × √ALT). AUROC 0.81. Non-invasive alternative to biopsy.
GRADE A Fibrosis Non-invasive
NAFLD-002
FIB-4 Index Validation: Advanced Fibrosis Detection
Author: Shah AG, et al.
Year: 2009
Journal: Hepatology
Design: Cross-sectional
Key Finding: FIB-4 AUROC 0.81-0.88 for advanced fibrosis. Outperforms APRI. Cost-effective, broadly applicable.

NAFLD-003
Insulin Resistance as Common Driver of NAFLD-MetS
Author: Stefan N, et al.
Year: 2018
Journal: Nat Rev
Design: Review
Key Finding: IR central to NAFLD. Links NAFLD-MetS-DM. Dietary intervention targets IR. HOMA-IR for risk.
GRADE B Pathophysiology IR Marker
📈 Metabolic Syndrome
MET-001
IDF Definition of Metabolic Syndrome 2019
Author: IDF Consensus Group
Year: 2019
Org: Int'l Diabetes Fdn
Design: Consensus
Key Finding: MetS = Central obesity + 2 of: HTN, dyslipidemia, hyperglycemia, low HDL. CVD risk 2-4x, DM 5-9x.

MetS & CVD Risk: Meta-Analysis
Author: Mottillo S, et al.
Year: 2010
Journal: JACC
Design: Meta-Analysis, 87
Key Finding: MetS diagnosis increases CVD 2.35x (RR 2.35, 95% CI 2.02-2.74). Mortality 1.52x.
GRADE A CVD Risk Meta-Analysis
MET-003
PREDIMED Study: Mediterranean Diet & CVD
Author: Estruch R, et al.
Year: 2013
Journal: NEJM
Design: RCT, 7,447
Key Finding: Mediterranean diet reduces CVD 30% in MetS. Primary prevention. Olive oil, nuts, whole grains.
GRADE A RCT Dietary
