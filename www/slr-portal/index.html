🎯 Key Findings
Dyslipidemia: 3 Grade A papers confirm lipid disorders affect 39% of adults; dyslipidemia among top 3 modifiable CVD risk factors. Dietary factors account for 30-40% of lipid variance.

Hypertension: 3 Grade A papers establish BP-CVD relationship; intensive control (SBP <120) reduces CVD 27% in high-risk groups (SPRINT trial).

NAFLD: 2 Grade A + 1 Grade B papers validate FIB-4 index (AUROC 0.81) for non-invasive fibrosis assessment; insulin resistance as common driver identified.

Metabolic Syndrome: 3 Grade A papers define 5-component diagnostic criteria; MetS increases CVD risk 2-4x and diabetes risk 5-9x; Mediterranean diet reduces CVD 30%.

Type 2 Diabetes: 2 Grade A + 1 Grade B papers show intensive glycemic control reduces complications 54-63%; HbA1c ≥6.5% diagnostic; prediabetes reversible with lifestyle.
