Systematic Literature Review Protocol with PRISMA-2020 Compliance

Project Overview

Project Name: Evidence-Based Metabolic Disease Screening Framework for Vitalora

Objective: Conduct a systematic literature review to identify, evaluate, and synthesize research evidence for screening and risk assessment of 15 metabolic diseases

Target Application: Vitalora telemedicine platform (patient education, risk scoring, clinical decision support)

Review Type: Narrative systematic review with GRADE evidence quality assessment

PRISMA 2020 Compliance: Yes — Full 27-item checklist completed

Research Questions

Q1: What are the pathophysiological pathways and risk factor cascades for each of the 15 metabolic diseases?
Q2: Which biomarkers are most sensitive and specific for screening/early detection of each disease?
Q3: What evidence-based algorithms exist for risk stratification (0-100 risk scores) for each disease?
Q4: What are the landmark epidemiological studies providing prevalence and incidence data?
Q5: What clinical cutoff points are supported by current evidence for diagnosis/risk classification?
Q6: What are the evidence-based herbal/dietary interventions for each disease?
Q7: What is the quality of evidence (GRADE A-D) supporting current screening recommendations?
Q8: What research gaps exist in screening methodology for these diseases?

Inclusion & Exclusion Criteria

Inclusion Criteria

  • Study Design: Randomized controlled trials, cohort studies, case-control studies, systematic reviews, meta-analyses
  • Participant Population: Adults (≥18 years) with or at risk for the 15 target diseases
  • Outcomes: Biomarker definition, risk prediction, diagnostic criteria, prevalence, incidence, validation of screening tools
  • Publication Status: Peer-reviewed journal articles, published guidelines (WHO, ADA, ESC, etc.)
  • Language: English-language publications
  • Time Period: 1995-2026 (emphasis on landmark trials and recent systematic reviews)

Exclusion Criteria

  • Study Design: Opinion articles, editorials, case reports (unless landmark observations), narrative reviews
  • Population: Pediatric populations (<18 years), pregnant women (separate pathophysiology)
  • Outcomes: Non-standardized biomarkers, proprietary scoring systems not independently validated
  • Publication: Gray literature, unpublished dissertations, conference abstracts without full publication
  • Relevance: Studies on drug efficacy without diagnostic/screening focus

Search Strategy

Information Sources

  1. PubMed/MEDLINE — Primary medical literature database
  2. Cochrane Library — Systematic reviews and RCTs
  3. Web of Science — Citation tracking and impact metrics
  4. Scopus — Comprehensive multidisciplinary coverage
  5. Clinical Guidelines — WHO, ACC/AHA, ADA, ESC, EASL

(dyslipidemia OR hypercholesterolemia OR "lipid disorder*") AND (screening OR diagnosis OR risk assessment OR biomarker* OR prediction model*) AND (sensitivity OR specificity OR "diagnostic accuracy" OR AUROC OR validation)

Search Parameters

  • Time Period: 1995-2026
  • Language: English

Study Selection Process

Two-Stage Screening

Stage 1 — Title & Abstract Screening: Two independent reviewers screen titles and abstracts against inclusion criteria. Conflicts resolved by discussion or third reviewer.

Stage 2 — Full-Text Review: Retrieve full texts of potentially eligible studies. Two independent reviewers assess against detailed inclusion criteria. Document reasons for exclusion.

Quality Control Checkpoints

  • Minimum 90% inter-rater agreement on 10% random sample (Stage 1)
  • Kappa statistic ≥0.60 for full-text screening
  • All disagreements resolved by consensus or third reviewer

Data Extraction Framework

Standardized extraction form with 30+ data elements per study:

  • Study characteristics: Author, year, journal, country, DOI, study design, population size
  • Population details: Age range, gender distribution, inclusion/exclusion criteria
  • Outcomes: Primary and secondary outcomes, biomarkers, diagnostic cutoffs
  • Methods: Risk stratification algorithms, sensitivity/specificity, AUROC
  • Results: Main findings, prevalence/incidence data, modifiable risk factors
  • Funding & Bias: Funding source, conflict of interest, study quality (GRADE)
  • Applicability: Relevance to Vitalora platform, extraction confidence level
