Dyslipidemia: Evidence & Risk Factors

  • Nordestgaard Study (2016): Familial hypercholesterolemia affects 1:500 people globally; severely underdiagnosed (only 10%)
  • INTERHEART Study (2004): 9 modifiable risk factors account for 90% of MI risk; dyslipidemia among top 3
  • AHA Statistics (2015): Dyslipidemia prevalence 39% of adults; dietary factors account for 30-40% of lipid variance

Key Cutoffs

Optimal LDL: <100 mg/dL | Borderline High: 100-129 | High: ≥160

Risk Algorithm: INTERHEART Risk Score combines lipids + smoking + HTN + diabetes (AUROC 0.87)

Hypertension: BP Targets & Outcomes

  • ACC/AHA 2017 Guideline: Hypertension definition shifted from 140/90 to 130/80; reflects intensive control evidence
  • Framingham Study (44 years): Establishes dose-dependent BP-CVD relationship; 10-year risk calculator foundation
  • SPRINT Trial (9,361 subjects): Intensive BP control (SBP <120 vs. <140) reduces CVD events 27% in high-risk patients

BP Classification

Normal: <120/80 | Elevated: 120-129/<80 | HTN Stage 1: 130-139/80-89 | HTN Stage 2: ≥140/≥90

Modifiable Factors: Sodium ↓, weight ↓, DASH diet, exercise, alcohol ↓

NAFLD: Fibrosis Assessment & Risk

  • EASL Guideline (2016): FIB-4 index recommended as first-line non-invasive fibrosis assessment tool
  • FIB-4 Validation (2009): AUROC 0.81-0.88 for detecting advanced fibrosis; outperforms APRI
  • Insulin Resistance Review (2018): IR central to NAFLD pathophysiology; links NAFLD-MetS-Diabetes continuum

FIB-4 Index

Formula: (Age × AST) / (Platelet × √ALT)

Interpretation: <1.30 = low risk | 1.30-2.67 = indeterminate | >2.67 = high risk

Advantage: Calculated from routine labs; cost-effective; non-invasive

Metabolic Syndrome: Diagnosis & Risk

  • IDF Definition (2019): Central obesity + 2 of: HTN, dyslipidemia, hyperglycemia, low HDL
  • Mottillo Meta-Analysis (87 studies): MetS increases CVD risk 2.35x and all-cause mortality 1.52x
  • PREDIMED Trial (7,447 subjects): Mediterranean diet reduces CVD events 30% in MetS/high-risk populations

MetS Components (IDF)

Central Obesity: Waist >94cm (M), >80cm (F) | Triglycerides: ≥150 mg/dL | HDL: <40 (M), <50 (F)

Blood Pressure: ≥130/85 mmHg | FPG: ≥100 mg/dL

Type 2 Diabetes: Diagnosis & Prevention

  • DCCT/EDIC (30-year RCT): Intensive glycemic control (HbA1c <7%) reduces complications 54-76%
  • ADA Standards (2024): T2DM diagnosis: FPG ≥126 | OGTT ≥200 | HbA1c ≥6.5%
  • Insulin Resistance Definition: Central mechanism in T2DM; reversible with lifestyle intervention (diet, exercise)

Screening & Prevention

Screen at: Age ≥45 or age 18-44 with BMI ≥25 + risk factor(s)

Prediabetes: HbA1c 5.7-6.4% or FPG 100-125 → Intensive lifestyle intervention (DPP study)

Risk Score: FINDRISC (8 factors); validated for diabetes prediction

Evidence-Based Risk Algorithms

  • Framingham Risk Score: 10-year CVD risk = f(age, BP, cholesterol, smoking, diabetes)
  • INTERHEART Risk Score: MI risk combines 9 factors; accounts for 90% of MI risk globally
  • FIB-4 Index: (Age × AST) / (Platelets × √ALT) — non-invasive NAFLD fibrosis assessment
  • FINDRISC: 8-factor diabetes risk calculator (age, BMI, family history, activity, diet, medication)
  • IDF Metabolic Syndrome Criteria: 5-component cluster (central obesity + 2 of: HTN, dyslipidemia, hyperglycemia, low HDL)

Research Gaps: 5 Major Opportunities

  • 1. Asymptomatic Screening Integration: Limited prospective validation in screening (asymptomatic) cohorts; need sensor-integrated risk models
  • 2. Herbal Medicine Evidence: Most traditional remedies lack rigorous RCTs; need evidence-based formulation guidelines
  • 3. Ethnic-Specific Validation: Screening cutoffs developed in Caucasian cohorts; need Indonesian/Asian population validation
  • 4. Wearable Sensor Validation: Smartwatch HR, SpO2, activity data not yet clinically validated for screening
  • 5. Implementation Science: Real-world screening uptake, patient adherence, physician integration understudied in low-resource settings

Gap Analysis: Herbal & Ethnic-Specific Focus

Herbal Medicine: HIGH Severity Gap

  • Issue: Curcumin (8 RCTs, Grade B-C), Garlic (6 RCTs, Grade C), Green tea (3 RCTs, Grade B)
  • Problem: Limited RCT evidence; outcome heterogeneity; potential medication interactions
  • Opportunity: Conduct rigorous RCTs for popular Indonesian herbal formulas (jamu); validate traditional medicine scientifically

Ethnic-Specific Validation: HIGH Severity Gap

  • Issue: FINDRISC, FIB-4, BP thresholds from Caucasian/Asian cohorts; unknown accuracy in Indonesians
  • Problem: Baseline prevalences, metabolic patterns, genetic variants differ across populations
  • Opportunity: Prospective cohort (n=5,000+) validating algorithms in Indonesian population; recalibrate cutoffs
