💛 Dyslipidemia Evidence
Nordestgaard Study (2016)
Familial hypercholesterolemia affects 1:500 globally; only 10% diagnosed
INTERHEART Study (2004)
9 modifiable risk factors account for 90% MI risk; dyslipidemia top 3. AUROC 0.87
AHA Statistics (2015)
39% of adults with dyslipidemia; dietary factors 30-40% of variance
LDL Cutoffs: Optimal <100 | Borderline 100-129 | High ≥160 mg/dL
Risk Algorithm: INTERHEART combines lipids + smoking + HTN + DM
❤️‍🩹 Hypertension Evidence
ACC/AHA 2017 Guideline
Shifted from 140/90 to 130/80; reflects intensive control evidence
Framingham Study (44 years)
Dose-dependent BP-CVD relationship; 10-year risk calculator foundation
SPRINT Trial (9,361 subjects)
Intensive SBP <120 vs <140 reduces CVD 27% in high-risk
Normal: <120/80 | Elevated: 120-129/<80 | HTN-1: 130-139/80-89 | HTN-2: ≥140/≥90
Modifiable: Sodium ↓, weight ↓, DASH diet, exercise, alcohol ↓
🏥 NAFLD Evidence
EASL Guideline (2016)
FIB-4 index first-line non-invasive fibrosis assessment
FIB-4 Validation (2009)
AUROC 0.81-0.88 for advanced fibrosis; outperforms APRI
Insulin Resistance Review (2018)
IR central to NAFLD; links NAFLD-MetS-Diabetes continuum
FIB-4 Formula: (Age × AST) / (Platelet × √ALT)
Interpretation: <1.30 = low | 1.30-2.67 = indeterminate | >2.67 = high
📈 Metabolic Syndrome Evidence
IDF Definition (2019)
Central obesity + ≥2 of: HTN, dyslipidemia, hyperglycemia, low HDL
Mottillo Meta-Analysis (87 studies)
MetS increases CVD 2.35x and mortality 1.52x
PREDIMED Trial (7,447 subjects)
Mediterranean diet reduces CVD 30% in MetS/high-risk
Central Obesity: Waist >94cm (M), >80cm (F)
Other: TG ≥150 | HDL <40(M)/<50(F) | BP ≥130/85 | FPG ≥100
🩺 Type 2 Diabetes Evidence
DCCT/EDIC (30-year RCT)
Intensive control (HbA1c <7%) reduces complications 54-76%
ADA Standards (2024)
Diagnosis: FPG ≥126 | OGTT ≥200 | HbA1c ≥6.5%
Insulin Resistance Definition
Central in T2DM; reversible with lifestyle (diet, exercise)
Screen at: Age ≥45 or 18-44 with BMI ≥25 + risk factor
Prediabetes: HbA1c 5.7-6.4% or FPG 100-125 → lifestyle intervention
Risk Score: FINDRISC (8 factors) for diabetes prediction

Key Takeaways
  • ✅ All 15 papers Grade A-B: High-quality evidence base supports platform implementation
  • ✅ Algorithms validated: Framingham, INTERHEART, FIB-4, FINDRISC all evidence-based
  • ✅ Multi-disease approach: Integrated risk assessment feasible
  • ⚠️ Ethnic validation needed: Cutoffs developed in Caucasian cohorts; validate in Indonesian populations
  • ⚠️ Wearable integration pending: Smartwatch data not yet clinically validated for screening
  • ⚠️ Herbal evidence limited: RCTs needed for traditional medicine recommendations
