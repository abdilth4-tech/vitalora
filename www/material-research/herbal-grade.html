Curcumin / Turmeric Quality

✅ RECOMMENDED (GRADE B)

Consistent evidence across 12 RCTs for dyslipidemia, type 2 diabetes, and systemic inflammation reduction.

Domain Rating Evidence Detail
Risk of Bias Low-Moderate Most studies double-blinded with ITT analysis.
Inconsistency Low Consistent LDL ↓15-25% and FPG ↓10-15%.
Indirectness Low Direct application to metabolic patient populations.
Imprecision Moderate CIs are narrow but sample sizes mostly n=80-150.
Publication Bias Possible Small negative trials may be underreported.
⚠️ Drug Interactions: May enhance effects of anticoagulants (Warfarin) and diabetes medications. Monitor glucose/INR.

Green Tea / EGCG Quality

✅ RECOMMENDED (GRADE B)

Landmark RCT evidence (n=143) showing weight reduction and metabolic component improvement.

Domain Rating Evidence Detail
Risk of Bias Low High-quality landmark trial (270 citations).
Inconsistency Low Weight loss 1-3kg consistent in multiple RCTs.
Inconsistency (Genotype) Moderate COMT genotype Val158Met predicts higher insulin response.

Garlic Extract Quality

✅ RECOMMENDED (GRADE B)

Aged Garlic Extract shows consistent SBP reduction of 4-8 mmHg in hypertensive patients.

Domain Rating Evidence Detail
Risk of Bias Low Proper blinding confirmable in landmark AGE-at-Heart trial.
Inconsistency Low-Mod BP effect consistent; extract standardization critical.

Cinnamon Quality

⚠️ ADJUNCTIVE USE (GRADE C)

Significant variability in results; approximately 30% of subjects identified as non-responders.

Domain Rating Evidence Detail
Inconsistency High Mixed results across 8 RCTs; responders vs non-responders.
Publication Bias Likely Selective outcome reporting for glycemic parameters.
❌ Note: High coumarin content in Cassia species may be hepatotoxic at high doses. Ceylon cinnamon preferred.
