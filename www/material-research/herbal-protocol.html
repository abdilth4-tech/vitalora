Systematic Literature Review Protocol for Herbal Medicine in Metabolic Disease

Project Overview

Project Name: Evidence-Based Herbal & Traditional Medicine Review for Metabolic Disease Management

Objective: Systematic evaluation of herbal remedies, traditional formulas, and phytochemical compounds for screening, prevention, and management of 5 metabolic diseases

Target Application: Vitalora herbal recommendation engine with evidence transparency

Review Type: Narrative systematic review with GRADE evidence quality assessment

PRISMA 2020 Compliance: Yes — Full 27-item checklist applied to herbal context

Research Questions

Q1: What herbal remedies and traditional formulas have evidence-based efficacy for dyslipidemia, hypertension, NAFLD, metabolic syndrome, and type 2 diabetes?
Q2: Which specific phytochemical compounds (curcumin, EGCG, allicin, etc.) show clinical benefit for metabolic disease outcomes?
Q3: What are the optimal dosages, durations, and formulations based on RCT evidence?
Q4: What is the quality of evidence (GRADE A-D) supporting herbal recommendations?
Q5: What are the safety profiles, contraindications, and drug interactions?
Q6: How do herbal remedies compare to pharmaceutical interventions in efficacy?
Q7: What mechanisms of action are validated in human clinical trials?
Q8: What are the research gaps for herbal medicine in metabolic disease management?

Inclusion & Exclusion Criteria

Inclusion Criteria

  • Study Design: Randomized controlled trials, controlled clinical trials, cohort studies, systematic reviews, meta-analyses
  • Intervention: Herbal preparations, plant extracts, isolated phytochemical compounds
  • Participant Population: Adults (≥18 years) with metabolic disease or at-risk populations
  • Outcomes: Biomarker changes (lipids, glucose, liver enzymes), clinical endpoints, safety
  • Publication Status: Peer-reviewed journal articles, published clinical guidelines
  • Language: English-language publications
  • Time Period: 2010-2026 (emphasis on recent RCT evidence)

Exclusion Criteria

  • Study Design: In vitro studies only, animal studies without human translation, case reports, opinions
  • Intervention: Untested proprietary formulas without mechanism validation
  • Population: Pediatric populations (<18 years), pregnancy-specific formulas
  • Outcomes: Non-standardized or unvalidated biomarkers
  • Publication: Gray literature without peer review

Search Parameters

  • Time Period: 2010-2026
  • Language: English
  • Focus: Standardized extracts and human clinical trials

Study Selection Process

Two-Stage Screening

Stage 1 — Title & Abstract Screening: Automated and manual screening for herbal intervention + metabolic disease keywords. Priority given to RCTs and meta-analyses.

Stage 2 — Full-Text Review: Retrieve full texts to assess against inclusion criteria. Extract study quality using GRADE assessment.

Quality Control

  • 90% inter-rater agreement target
  • Systematic documentation of exclusion reasons
  • Focus on standardization of herbal interventions

Data Extraction Framework

Standardized extraction form with 25+ data elements per study:

  • Study characteristics: Author, year, journal, DOI, study design, population size, duration
  • Intervention: Herbal name (scientific), extract type, active compounds, dosage (mg/day), comparator
  • Outcomes: Biomarker changes (LDL, HDL, BP, Glucose, ALT), effect sizes, statistical significance
  • Safety: Adverse events documented, drug-herb interactions, contraindications
  • Evidence Quality: GRADE rating (A-D), risk of bias assessment

GRADE Quality Categories

  • Grade A (High): Multiple RCTs, consistent effects, large sample sizes (highest certainty)
  • Grade B (Moderate): RCT with limitations OR multiple smaller trials, some inconsistency
  • Grade C (Low): Few RCTs, small sample sizes, inconsistent results (common for herbal)
  • Grade D (Very Low): Case series, observational only, high risk of bias
