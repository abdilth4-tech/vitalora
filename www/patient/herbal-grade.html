Clinical Efficacy
• Dyslipidemia: LDL ↓15-25%
• T2 Diabetes: FPG ↓10-15%, HbA1c ↓7-8%
• NAFLD: ALT ↓25-30%
• Inflammation: CRP ↓20-35%

✅ RECOMMENDED for Dyslipidemia, T2DM, MetS

• Central Obesity: WC ↓2.4 cm

🟤 Cinnamon
Grade C
WHO Reference: Monographs Vol. 3 (2007) | RCTs: 8 studies | Note: Variable glucose response
⚠️ CAUTION — Contraindication Alert:
Coumarin-sensitive patients: Cassia cinnamon contains high coumarin (6-7 mg/tsp) which inhibits CYP2C9 → increased warfarin effect, bleeding risk.
Recommendation: Use Ceylon cinnamon only (0.004 mg coumarin/tsp) if patient on anticoagulants OR avoid entirely.
5-Domain GRADE Assessment
Risk of Bias
Moderate (small trials)
Inconsistency
Moderate-High (variable response)
Indirectness
Moderate (mixed populations)
Imprecision
High (wide CIs, small n)
Publication Bias
Likely (positive studies published)
Clinical Efficacy
• FPG Reduction: 24.7 mg/dL (meta-analysis)
• HbA1c: ↓0.54% (variable)
• Effect heterogeneity: 30-70% responder variability
Dose: 1-6 g/day (variable efficacy)
⚠️ ADJUNCTIVE ONLY — Evidence weak, use with caution in anticoagulation
