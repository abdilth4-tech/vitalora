📊 GRADE Quality Assessment Framework
Grade A (High Quality): Further research unlikely to change effect estimate. Multiple RCTs, consistent results, large sample sizes.

Grade B (Moderate Quality): Further research may change effect estimate. Well-designed trials, some heterogeneity, medium sample sizes.

Grade C (Low Quality): Further research very likely to change effect estimate. Limited RCTs, observational studies, small sample sizes.

Grade D (Very Low Quality): Any evidence estimate uncertain. Case reports, mechanistic studies, poorly designed trials.

5 GRADE Domains Assessed:
  • Risk of Bias (study design quality, randomization, blinding)
  • Inconsistency (heterogeneity, results variation across studies)
  • Indirectness (relevance to target population)
  • Imprecision (confidence intervals, sample size)
  • Publication Bias (funnel plot, selective reporting)
⚠️ Safety & Drug Interaction Assessment
Safety Domains Assessed Per Herbal:
• Adverse event profiles (frequency, severity)
• Contraindications (pregnancy, liver disease, renal impairment)
• Drug-herb interactions (CYP3A4/2C9 induction/inhibition)
• Dosage safety limits (maximum daily intake)
• Special populations (pediatric, elderly, renal/hepatic disease)

Information Sources: WHO Herbal Monographs, published RCTs, pharmacovigilance data, FDA/EMA safety reports
