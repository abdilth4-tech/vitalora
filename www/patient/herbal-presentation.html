🟡 CURCUMIN / TURMERIC

Active Compound: Curcuminoid (3-7% in rhizome)

WHO Status: Monographs Vol. 4 — Accepted for anti-inflammatory use

Grade B Evidence — 12 RCTs
LDL ↓15-25% | FPG ↓10-15% | ALT ↓25-30% | CRP ↓20-35%

Optimal Dose: 500-1000 mg/day with piperine (enhances absorption 2000%)

Safety: Well-tolerated; caution with anticoagulants & diabetes meds (hypoglycemia risk)

🟢 GREEN TEA / EGCG

Active Compound: EGCG (epigallocatechin gallate) — potent polyphenol antioxidant

WHO Status: Monographs Vol. 1 — Strong traditional & clinical evidence

Grade A Evidence — 15 RCTs + 3 Meta-analyses
Weight Loss ↓1-3 kg | LDL ↓5-8% | MetS ↓18% | Adiponectin ↑18%

Optimal Dose: 300-400 mg EGCG/day (= 3-5 cups tea or standardized extract)

Note: May enhance warfarin; avoid with iron supplements

⚪ GARLIC

Active Compound: Allicin (enzymatic breakdown of alliin)

WHO Status: Monographs Vol. 1 — Traditional antihypertensive use

Grade B Evidence — 10+ RCTs
SBP ↓4-8 mmHg | DBP ↓2-3 mmHg | LDL ↓7% | TG ↓8% | ApoB ↓7%

Optimal Form: Aged garlic extract (higher allicin content, better stability)

Dose: 600-900 mg aged garlic/day

Note: Enhanced antiplatelet effect; monitor if combined with aspirin/NSAIDs

🟠 GINGER

FPG ↓10.3% | HbA1c ↓0.76% | CRP ↓18% | Anti-inflammatory biomarkers

Optimal Dose: 1-4 g/day fresh or dried

Benefits: Glucose control + inflammation reduction + GI comfort

Grade B Evidence — 6 RCTs

🟤 CINNAMON — ⚠️ CAUTION

Active Compound: Cinnamaldehyde + coumarin (variable by species)

WHO Status: Monographs Vol. 3 — Traditional use (NOT glucose-specific)

Grade C Evidence — 8 RCTs, HIGH HETEROGENEITY
FPG ↓24.7 mg/dL (variable: 10-60% responders) | HbA1c ↓0.54%

⚠️ CRITICAL WARNING — Coumarin Interaction:

  • Cassia cinnamon: 6-7 mg coumarin/tsp → AVOID if on warfarin/anticoagulants
  • Ceylon cinnamon: 0.004 mg coumarin/tsp → SAFE choice for anticoagulation

Grade C Recommendation: Use only if Ceylon cinnamon & not on anticoagulants

GRADE Evidence Quality Framework

Grade A (High Quality)
Further research unlikely to change effect. Multiple RCTs, consistent results, large n. Example: Green Tea EGCG
Grade B (Moderate Quality)
Further research may change effect. 3-4 RCTs, some limitations, n>100. Example: Curcumin, Garlic, Ginger, Olive Leaf
Grade C (Low Quality)
Further research very likely to change effect. <2 RCTs, small n, inconsistent. Example: Cinnamon, Jamu
13 / 20

5-Domain GRADE Assessment

Each herbal assessed on 5 domains:

  • Risk of Bias: Study design quality, randomization, blinding
  • Inconsistency: Heterogeneity across studies, effect direction consistency
  • Indirectness: Population relevance, outcome relevance to clinical question
  • Imprecision: Confidence intervals, adequate sample sizes
  • Publication Bias: Selective outcome reporting, missing negative studies

Result: Overall grade (A, B, C, D) and recommendations for clinical use

Safety Profiles & Drug Interactions

Curcumin: Enhanced effect with anticoagulants; potentiates diabetes meds (hypoglycemia risk)

Green Tea: May enhance warfarin; avoid with iron supplements (absorption interference)

Garlic: Enhanced antiplatelet effect with aspirin/NSAIDs; increased warfarin effect

Ginger: Enhanced antiplatelet/anticoagulant effect — monitor bleeding risk

Cinnamon (Cassia): ⚠️ CRITICAL — Coumarin inhibits CYP2C9 → bleeding if on warfarin

Olive Leaf: Additive BP-lowering with antihypertensives (monitor BP)

Research Gaps & Future Directions

  • Jamu Standardization: Need for GMP compliance & formulation consistency
  • Long-term Safety: Limited data on 12-24 month herbal use in metabolic disease
  • Genetic Stratification: COMT genotype effects on green tea response — personalized dosing
  • Mechanism Validation: Human mechanistic studies (not just animal models)
  • Combination Efficacy: Herbal + pharmacotherapy interaction studies needed
  • Population Diversity: More RCTs in non-Asian populations
